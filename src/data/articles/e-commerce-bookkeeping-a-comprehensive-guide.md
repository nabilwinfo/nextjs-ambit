## The Basics of E-commerce Bookkeeping

You must take several necessary steps to set up a bookkeeping workflow for an e-commerce store. A simplified version of this process might look something like this:

1. Determine how you want to track and cost your inventory
2. Download retail accounting software
3. Create templates for sales orders, invoices, and receipts
4. Start tracking your inventory
5. Create a balance sheet for your first month of business. Create an income statement and a cash flow statement too.

## Keeping Track of All Income and Expenses:

In the fast-paced world of ecommerce, it's vital to stay on top of your income and expenses. Neglecting to do so can lead to financial chaos and missed opportunities. Here's how to stay organized with best practices for total revenue and net profit.

### 1. Maintain detailed records of all transactions:

Track all sources of income, including sales revenue, fees, and shipping costs. Also track any other expenses associated with your business. Maintaining meticulous records allows you to reconcile accounts and ensure accuracy. You can categorize your expenses. This lets you find ways to cut costs and make more profit using cash basis accounting.

### 2. Embrace accounting software:

Use accounting software like QuickBooks Online or Xero. It will simplify your bookkeeping process. These tools automate data entry. They also generate reports and provide real-time insights into your financials. Integrate your ecommerce platform with your accounting software, such as bigcommerce. This way, you can import sales data. You can also automate transaction recording and save valuable time. With an online store, you can manage your sales and inventory in one place. It's easier to track your finances and grow your business.

## Separating Personal and Business Finances:

To maintain financial clarity and cut complications, it's essential to keep your personal and business finances separate. Follow these guidelines for managing your personal finances.

### 1. Open a dedicated business bank account and credit card:

Separate business accounts from personal ones to avoid commingling funds. This separation streamlines your bookkeeping, simplifies tax reporting, and safeguards your personal assets. Also, having a separate business bank account and credit card helps you track your business expenses more. This is especially true if you are an ecommerce business owner. You are looking for ecommerce books.

### 2. Create a budget and stick to it:

Develop a well-defined budget for your business. Track your expenses and income against this budget. Identify areas where you spend too much. You can make informed decisions and ensure pricing profitability. A budget serves as a financial roadmap. It helps you divide resources and maintain a healthy bottom line. Taxes are crucial for budgeting. Consult the [IRS](https://www.irs.gov/) or a tax professional to ensure compliance and avoid penalties.

## Tracking Fees Charged by Ecommerce Platform and Shipping Costs

As an **e-commerce** business owner, it is important to track all fees charged by your e-commerce platform. You should also track shipping costs. These expenses can add up and impact your profit margins if not managed .

To track these costs, you can use accounting software that integrates with your ecommerce platform. This allows you to import transaction data. You can categorize fees and shipping costs from sales revenue. By doing so, you can identify these expenses and track their impact on your profitability in real-time.

Another important aspect to consider is reconciling your accounts . This involves comparing and verifying transactions between your ecommerce platform and accounting software. It ensures accuracy and prevents discrepancies. By reconciling your accounts, you can identify any errors or missing transactions and correct them .

It is also important to keep detailed records of all expenses related to your e-commerce business. This includes receipts and invoices. This not only ensures compliance with tax regulations, but also helps with financial analysis and budgeting.
